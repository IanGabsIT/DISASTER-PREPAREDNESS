Ways to be Prepared

Volcanic Eruption Preparedness Strategies

Before Eruption

  • Know your proximity to active volcanoes
  • Monitor PHIVOLCS alert levels regularly
  • Understand alert level meanings (0-5)
  • Prepare emergency kit with 7-day supplies
  • Stock N95 masks, goggles, and protective gear
  • Identify evacuation routes and centers

During Ashfall

  • Stay indoors and close all windows and doors
  • Wear N95 mask and protective goggles
  • Cover nose and mouth when going outside
  • Protect water sources from ash contamination
  • Avoid driving - ash reduces visibility
  • Listen to radio for official updates

Evacuation & Recovery

  • Evacuate immediately when Alert Level 4-5 is declared
  • Follow designated evacuation routes
  • Bring go-bag with essentials
  • Do not return until authorities permit
  • Clean ash from roofs carefully
  • Be aware of lahar flow risks after eruption

Ways to Mitigate Accidents

Volcanic Eruption Accident Mitigation Strategies

Structural & Technical

  • Build lahar diversion channels and dikes
  • Install early warning systems and monitoring
  • Construct evacuation centers outside danger zones
  • Protect critical infrastructure from ashfall
  • Install air filtration systems in buildings
  • Develop ashfall removal and disposal systems

Behavioral & Policy

  • Enforce permanent danger zone restrictions
  • Conduct regular evacuation drills
  • Public education on PHIVOLCS alert levels
  • Coordination between PHIVOLCS and LGUs
  • Community volcano monitoring programs
  • Establish clear evacuation procedures

Cause and Effect

Volcanic Eruption: Causes and Effects

Primary Causes Immediate Effects Long-term Consequences
Magma Pressure: Accumulation of molten rock beneath surface Explosive eruptions, ashfall, pyroclastic flows, lava flows Climate impact, agricultural damage, displacement, tourism decline
Tectonic Activity: Subduction zones, plate movements Volcanic earthquakes, ground deformation, gas emissions Ongoing volcanic activity, continued monitoring needs
Gas Buildup: Trapped volcanic gases in magma Explosive eruptions, ash clouds, toxic gas release Air quality issues, health impacts, environmental damage
Lahar Formation: Volcanic material mixing with water Mudflows, river channel blockage, flooding Long-term lahar risk, permanent landscape changes, relocation
