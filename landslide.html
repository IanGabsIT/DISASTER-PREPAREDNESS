Ways to be Prepared

Landslide Preparedness Strategies

Prevention Measures

  • Check MGB landslide hazard maps for your area
  • Avoid building on steep slopes or unstable ground
  • Plant vegetation to stabilize slopes
  • Install proper drainage systems
  • Monitor slope stability regularly
  • Report warning signs to authorities immediately

Warning Signs to Watch

  • Cracks in ground, walls, or roads
  • Bulging ground at base of slope
  • Water seeping from slope
  • Tilting trees, utility poles, or fences
  • Sudden changes in water flow
  • Unusual sounds (rumbling, cracking)

During Landslide Risk

  • Evacuate immediately if authorities recommend
  • Move to higher ground away from slopes
  • Stay away from landslide paths
  • Listen to radio for updates
  • Be alert during heavy rainfall
  • Do not return until authorities say it's safe

Ways to Mitigate Accidents

Landslide Accident Mitigation Strategies

Structural & Technical

  • Install retaining walls and slope stabilization
  • Construct proper drainage systems
  • Build terraces on steep slopes
  • Install early warning systems and sensors
  • Use geotextiles and slope reinforcement
  • Implement slope monitoring systems

Behavioral & Policy

  • Enforce no-build zones in high-risk areas
  • Promote reforestation and vegetation cover
  • Regulate mining and quarrying activities
  • Community landslide awareness programs
  • Regular slope inspections and maintenance
  • Coordination with MGB for hazard mapping

Cause and Effect

Landslide: Causes and Effects

Primary Causes Immediate Effects Long-term Consequences
Heavy Rainfall: Saturated soil, loss of slope stability Mass movement of soil and rocks, burial of structures, blocked roads Permanent displacement, infrastructure damage, watershed degradation
Earthquakes: Ground shaking destabilizing slopes Sudden landslides, multiple slope failures, widespread damage Long-term slope instability, relocation needs, economic losses
Human Activities: Deforestation, mining, construction Slope destabilization, increased erosion, triggered landslides Environmental degradation, increased vulnerability, policy changes
Volcanic Activity: Lahar flows, ash accumulation Volcanic landslides, lahar flows, debris avalanches Long-term lahar risk, permanent relocation, ecosystem changes
