Ways to be Prepared

Earthquake Preparedness Strategies

Before an Earthquake

  • Secure heavy furniture, appliances, and fixtures to walls
  • Store breakable items on lower shelves
  • Install latches on cabinet doors
  • Anchor water heater and gas appliances
  • Prepare emergency supply kit (72-hour minimum)
  • Learn how to shut off gas, water, and electricity

During an Earthquake

  • DROP to the ground immediately
  • Take COVER under sturdy furniture or against interior wall
  • HOLD ON until shaking stops
  • Stay away from windows, glass, and heavy objects
  • If outdoors, move to open area away from buildings
  • If in vehicle, stop in safe area and stay inside

After an Earthquake

  • Check for injuries and provide first aid
  • Check for gas leaks and shut off if needed
  • Inspect utilities and shut off if damaged
  • Listen to battery-powered radio for updates
  • Be prepared for aftershocks
  • Avoid damaged buildings and areas

Ways to Mitigate Accidents

Earthquake Accident Mitigation Strategies

Structural & Technical

  • Retrofit buildings with base isolators and shear walls
  • Follow Building Code standards for seismic design
  • Install flexible gas and water connections
  • Use earthquake-resistant construction materials
  • Secure building foundations and structural elements
  • Install automatic gas shutoff valves

Behavioral & Policy

  • Conduct regular earthquake drills in schools and workplaces
  • Enforce building code compliance
  • Establish early warning systems
  • Community earthquake preparedness programs
  • Regular inspection of infrastructure
  • Public education on earthquake safety

Cause and Effect

Earthquake: Causes and Effects

Primary Causes Immediate Effects Long-term Consequences
Tectonic Plate Movement: Philippine Fault System, subduction zones Ground shaking, building collapse, landslides, liquefaction Infrastructure damage, economic loss, displacement, PTSD
Volcanic Activity: Magma movement beneath volcanoes Volcanic earthquakes, ground deformation Volcanic eruption risk, evacuation needs
Human Activities: Mining, reservoir-induced seismicity Minor to moderate shaking in specific areas Structural damage, environmental impact
Tsunami Generation: Undersea earthquakes Large ocean waves, coastal flooding Widespread destruction, loss of life, coastal damage
