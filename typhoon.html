Ways to be Prepared

Typhoon Preparedness Strategies

Before Typhoon Season

  • Reinforce roof and secure loose materials
  • Install storm shutters or prepare window coverings
  • Trim trees and remove dead branches
  • Clear gutters and drainage systems
  • Prepare emergency supply kit (7-day minimum)
  • Identify evacuation routes and centers

When Typhoon Approaches

  • Monitor PAGASA bulletins and warnings
  • Secure outdoor furniture and objects
  • Fill containers with clean water
  • Charge all electronic devices
  • Prepare go-bag for evacuation
  • Stay indoors and away from windows

During and After Typhoon

  • Stay indoors until authorities say it's safe
  • Listen to battery-powered radio for updates
  • Avoid flooded areas and downed power lines
  • Check for injuries and provide first aid
  • Inspect utilities before using
  • Document damage for insurance claims

Ways to Mitigate Accidents

Typhoon Accident Mitigation Strategies

Structural & Technical

  • Build typhoon-resistant structures with reinforced roofs
  • Install storm shutters and impact-resistant windows
  • Improve drainage systems to prevent flooding
  • Construct sea walls and breakwaters for coastal areas
  • Install early warning systems and sirens
  • Strengthen power and communication infrastructure

Behavioral & Policy

  • Enforce building codes for typhoon resistance
  • Conduct regular community preparedness drills
  • Establish clear evacuation procedures
  • Maintain mangrove forests for natural protection
  • Public education on typhoon safety
  • Coordination between LGUs and national agencies
