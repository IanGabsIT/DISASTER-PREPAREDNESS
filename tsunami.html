Ways to be Prepared

Tsunami Preparedness Strategies

Before a Tsunami

  • Know your elevation and distance from coast
  • Identify evacuation routes to high ground (30+ meters)
  • Practice evacuation route with family
  • Register for tsunami early warning alerts
  • Learn natural warning signs (earthquake, sea withdrawal)
  • Prepare go-bag for quick evacuation

Natural Warning Signs

  • Strong earthquake that lasts 20+ seconds
  • Unusual sea behavior (rapid withdrawal)
  • Loud ocean roar or rumbling sound
  • Rapid rise or fall of coastal water
  • Official tsunami warning issued
  • Ground shaking near coast

During Tsunami Warning

  • Evacuate immediately - don't wait for official warning
  • Move to high ground (30+ meters) or inland
  • Go on foot if possible - avoid traffic
  • Never go to coast to watch tsunami
  • Stay away from rivers leading to sea
  • Follow vertical evacuation if in building

Ways to Mitigate Accidents

Tsunami Accident Mitigation Strategies

Structural & Technical

  • Build tsunami-resistant structures and sea walls
  • Install early warning systems and sirens
  • Create vertical evacuation structures
  • Establish clear evacuation routes and signage
  • Install tsunami detection buoys
  • Build breakwaters and coastal barriers

Behavioral & Policy

  • Enforce no-build zones in high-risk coastal areas
  • Conduct regular tsunami evacuation drills
  • Public education on natural warning signs
  • Establish clear evacuation procedures
  • Community tsunami awareness programs
  • Coordination between PHIVOLCS and coastal LGUs

Cause and Effect

Tsunami: Causes and Effects

Primary Causes Immediate Effects Long-term Consequences
Undersea Earthquakes: Subduction zone earthquakes displacing ocean floor High-speed ocean waves, coastal inundation, massive destruction Widespread property loss, displacement, economic devastation, PTSD
Volcanic Eruptions: Underwater volcanic activity Volcanic tsunamis, coastal flooding, debris impact Combined volcanic and tsunami damage, long recovery
Landslides: Underwater or coastal landslides Localized tsunamis, coastal impact, debris flows Coastal erosion, infrastructure damage, relocation
Meteorite Impact: Rare but possible ocean impact Massive waves, widespread coastal destruction Catastrophic damage, long-term environmental impact
